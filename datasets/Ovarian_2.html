The Ovarian Tumor Dataset

The OvaTUS dataset was collected by the research team of the Signal, Information, and Multimedia Content Processing Laboratory (SigM Lab) in collaboration with the National Hospital of Obstetrics and Gynecology (NHOG) in Hanoi, Vietnam, as part of the grant number KC-4.0-45/19-25 "Research and development of a computer-aided support system for ovarian cancer diagnosis using ultrasound images". This dataset comprises ultrasound images from women who visited the hospital for ovarian tumor assessment and consented to participate in the study. To ensure the effectiveness of data collection and analysis in research and pathological diagnosis, particularly in the management and evaluation of ovarian tumors via ultrasound, the documentation of essential data fields is crucial. These data fields not only facilitate the characterization and severity assessment of tumors but also support accurate and timely treatment decision-making. Detailed information regarding ovarian tumor characteristics is vital for diagnosis, severity assessment, and treatment planning.

Detail of the OvaTUS dataset

A. Data collection

OvaTUS-V1 is the first official release of our ovarian tumor ultrasound image dataset. This dataset is developed following a standardized pipeline for data collection, storage, and annotation, ensuring consistency and reliability across all samples. All procedures strictly comply with ethical guidelines for biomedical research involving human subjects. OvaTUS-V1 is designed to support ongoing and future research in medical image analysis and will continue to be expanded with additional cases and annotations, while preserving the integrity of the data and adherence to medical ethics throughout the dataset’s evolution. The process of collecting:

  1. Step 01: We recruit volunteers aged 20–70, inform them about the study, and obtain their consent to use their medical results.
  2. Step 02: Patients undergo preoperative CA125 and HE4 biomarker testing, along with ultrasound imaging. Only those diagnosed with organic ovarian tumors and indicated for surgery, with tumor evaluation based on the IOTA model, are included.
  3. Step 03: Patients with functional ovarian cysts, pregnancy, end-stage renal failure, or a history of organ transplantation are excluded.
  4. Step 04: Surgery and postoperative histopathological analysis confirm the tumor's final diagnosis. Samples from confirmed benign or malignant cases are retained, excluding those with discrepant pre- and postoperative diagnoses.
  5. Step 05: We apply techniques to remove markers, and patients' personal data, as well as to control the quality of the images before passing them for annotation. The final dataset is stored on a cloud server.

B. Data pre-processing and annotation

The captured images undergo pre-processing before annotation. This pre-processing step includes removing personal data and eliminating markers added by doctors during ultrasound imaging. We utilize the available IOPaint tool for inpainting the images, ensuring a clean and standardized dataset for annotation. Then the doctors use the LabelMe tool for tumor region annotation. LabelMe is a free and convenient tool developed by MIT CSCI labs that presents the LabelMe interface as used by doctors, where the red line delineates the tumor boundary. During the annotation process, doctors followed Vietnamese standards and consulted the IOTA standards for ovarian tumor classification, which includes 6 types of tumors: Solid tumor, Multilocular cyst, Unilocular cyst, Dermoid cyst, Multilocular-solid cyst, and Unilocular-solid cyst. The number of samples per class in the OVATUS-V1 dataset is summarized in the table below:

No. Category Description
1 Solid tumor A tumor with a cystic structure with only one lobe, without septa, solid tissue, or buds.
2 Multilobular cyst A cyst with at least one septation and no solid components or papillary projections.
3 Unilocular cyst A tumor with a cystic structure with only one lobe, but with the presence of a solid part or at least one bud.
4 Dermoid cyst Cyst with a solid appearance and thin walls, typically unilocular and exhibiting a ground-glass pattern.
5 Multilobular-solid cyst A single-locule ovarian cyst that contains a solid component or at least one papillary projection within its structure.
6 Unilocular-solid cyst A cyst with a single locule and a solid component or at least one papillary projection.

Note: This dataset is continuously updated with new cases and annotations.
